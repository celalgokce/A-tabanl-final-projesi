Friends

What is Friends About?
Friends is a series that follows the lively, humorous, and vibrant adventures of six individuals, each with different personalities, in their twenties living in New York City. It portrays their amusing antics at Central Perk Cafe, where they frequently hang out. The series starts with Rachel Green leaving her wedding and searching for her childhood friend Monica Geller at a restaurant in New York. After finding Monica, Rachel moves in with her and becomes part of her fun friend group. This group includes Phoebe Buffay, who works as both a masseuse and a musician, aspiring actor Joey Tribbiani, recently divorced paleontologist Ross Geller, and businessman Chandler Bing, who is also Monica's brother. These six characters engage in romantic relationships, some short-lived and some long-term.
Who Are the Cast Members of Friends?
Friends features an iconic cast led by Jennifer Aniston, along with Matt LeBlanc, Lisa Kudrow, Courteney Cox, David Schwimmer, and Matthew Perry. The show includes a total of 34 actors. With its impressive cast and compelling storyline, Friends stands out as one of the most popular series among young adults. You can enjoy watching this series on the Netflix platform.


How I Met Your Mother

HIMYM Summary and Details
We're in the year 2030; Ted Mosby is telling his kids the 'long' story of how he met their mother. Suddenly, we are taken back to the America of 2005, where we witness the fun lives of five tight-knit friends in their twenties. Ted, a 27-year-old architect from Manhattan, is best friends with Marshall, who is studying law and has been with his girlfriend Lily for nine years. The final member of the group, Barney Stinson, is a one-of-a-kind, suit-wearing womanizer. When Marshall tells Ted he's going to propose to Lily, Ted realizes he doesn’t want to remain single and believes he'll find his true love. Just then, he meets Robin Scherbatsky, completing the tight-knit group of How I Met Your Mother. The show, created by Carter Bays and Craig Thomas, stands out as one of the most beloved comedy sitcoms of recent times due to its time-jumping narrative, reflections of characters in daily life, and perspectives on male-female relationships.


Yalan Dünya

Yalan Dünya Summary and Details
The Kocabaş family patriarch Şehmuz, the meticulous and bleach-loving wife Servet, their son-in-law and mama's boy Selahattin, their daughter Gülistan, grandson Orçun, Orçun's girlfriend Eylem, and the unmarried son Rıza all live together. After oving from Antakya to Istanbul-Cihangir, the Kocabaş family is under the domination of patriarch Şehmuz Bey and their traditions. Selahattin, their only son, is not loyal to his wife Gülistan and is having an affair with Tülay, whom he met at a nightclub. Rıza, the only son, is engaged to Nurhayat against his will due to family pressure. Unable to endure this any longer, Rıza decides to break up with Nurhayat, causing turmoil in the Kocabaş family. The family tries to reconcile Rıza and Nurhayat. During these events, a life-changing incident occurs that affects the whole family. Deniz, accompanied by her brother Bora, comes from Izmir to Istanbul to make a guest appearance on a TV series. Deniz, who has been looking for an opportunity for years, suddenly gets lucky. The lead actor of the show causes trouble on set and quits. Deniz gets promoted from a guest role to the lead role, and she starts looking for a house in Istanbul. Another actor on set, Açılay, is also looking for a house and offers Deniz to become roommates. Şehmuz Bey gets mad at Rıza and decides to rent out the adjacent apartment after marrying Nurhayat. Everyone tries to get that apartment. While Selahattin plans to house his mistress Tülay there, Deniz, Açılay, and Bora come up with a plan to convince Şehmuz Bey to rent the apartment. However, things soon get out of control. On top of all this, Deniz's parents move from Izmir to Istanbul, making the situation even more chaotic.
